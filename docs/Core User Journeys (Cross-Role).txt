Core User Journeys (Cross-Role)

Real Estate Transaction CRM: Core End-to-End Journeys
1. Lead Intake → Closing → Post-Close Follow-up
Ownership: Realtor (primary), CRM System (automation)

Entry Point
Digital form submission (website, portal, app)

Manual entry by realtor (referral, open house, past client)

Integration from third-party lead source (Zillow, Realtor.com)

Key Decisions
Lead qualification criteria match (timeline, budget, motivation)

Assignment to appropriate realtor (specialty, capacity, geography)

Communication cadence establishment (automated vs personal)

Engagement tracking threshold for follow-up intensity

Role Handoffs
Marketing automation → Realtor (when lead scores above threshold)

Realtor → Transaction Coordinator (once property identified/listed)

Realtor → Post-Close Specialist (at closing)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Lead profile, communication log, engagement score, source attribution

Consumed: Market data, realtor performance metrics, response time benchmarks

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Manual data re-entry between lead source and CRM

Unclear lead ownership causing delayed response

Lost context between initial contact and transaction start

Inconsistent follow-up timing across team members

2. Buyer Journey
Ownership: Buyer (actor), Realtor (guide), Mortgage Broker (enabler)

Entry Point
Pre-approval document upload or mortgage application start

Property search criteria definition

First property tour scheduling

Key Decisions
Pre-approval amount acceptance and lender selection

Property must-haves vs nice-to-haves refinement

Offer strategy development (price, contingencies, timing)

Inspection objection prioritization and negotiation

Final walkthrough acceptance

Role Handoffs
Mortgage Broker → Realtor (pre-approval confirmation)

Realtor → Attorney (signed purchase agreement)

Buyer → Insurance Provider (binding request)

Attorney → Title Company (closing package)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Saved properties, offer history, inspection reports, contingencies

Consumed: Property listings, market comparables, loan estimates, inspection availability

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Repeated document requests from multiple parties

Lack of visibility into offer review timeline

Confusion about contingency deadlines

Last-minute closing cost surprises

Inconsistent communication about next steps

3. Seller Journey
Ownership: Seller (actor), Realtor (advisor), Subcontractor (preparer)

Entry Point
Listing agreement signing

Property valuation assessment

Pre-listing preparation checklist

Key Decisions
Listing price strategy based on market analysis

Repair/improvement investment decisions

Offer evaluation criteria (price, contingencies, closing timeline)

Counter-offer strategy

Post-inspection repair negotiation boundaries

Role Handoffs
Realtor → Photographer/Stager (listing preparation)

Realtor → Showing Assistant (access coordination)

Seller → Subcontractor (repair authorization)

Realtor → Buyer's Attorney (disclosure delivery)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Property disclosures, showing feedback, repair estimates, offer comparisons

Consumed: Market absorption rates, comparable sales, showing analytics, buyer qualification

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Unclear showing feedback aggregation

Delayed offer presentation

Manual coordination of repair estimates

Disorganized document disclosure tracking

Last-minute closing delays due to title issues

4. Realtor Transaction Management
Ownership: Realtor (orchestrator), Transaction Coordinator (executor)

Entry Point
Mutual acceptance of purchase agreement

Transaction checklist auto-generation

Key Decisions
Contingency management sequencing (inspection → loan → appraisal)

Communication protocol establishment with all parties

Deadline monitoring and enforcement strategy

Problem escalation path when issues arise

Commission split and disbursement tracking

Role Handoffs
Realtor → Transaction Coordinator (day-to-day management)

Realtor → Attorney (legal document review)

Realtor → Other Agent (professional courtesy updates)

Listing Agent → Buyer's Agent (key information sharing)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Transaction timeline, task assignments, deadline alerts, communication logs

Consumed: Contract terms, contingency statuses, role contact information, document status

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Manual deadline tracking across multiple systems

Unclear responsibility assignment for tasks

Inconsistent status updates to clients

Missing document chain-of-custody

Last-minute scramble for missing signatures

5. Attorney Closing Workflow
Ownership: Closing Attorney (legal authority), Title Company (data source)

Entry Point
Executed purchase agreement receipt

Title order placement

Key Decisions
Title issue resolution path (cure vs insurance)

Document exception negotiation

Escrow instruction finalization

Funding release authorization

Recording priority and method

Role Handoffs
Attorney → Title Company (title search request)

Attorney → Lender (closing disclosure approval)

Attorney → Realtor (closing statement review)

Attorney → Recording Office (document submission)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Closing statements, title commitments, settlement statements, escrow instructions

Consumed: Purchase agreement, loan documents, payoff statements, tax information

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Incomplete payoff information from existing lenders

Missing signatures on critical documents

Last-minute changes to closing costs

Unclear funding timelines between parties

Manual calculation errors in settlement statements

6. Mortgage & Insurance Coordination
Ownership: Mortgage Broker (primary), Insurance Provider (parallel), Underwriter (gatekeeper)

Entry Point
Formal loan application submission

Insurance quote request

Key Decisions
Loan program selection and locking strategy

Document exception handling approach

Appraisal review and challenge decision

Clear-to-close conditions verification

Insurance binder timing coordination

Role Handoffs
Mortgage Broker → Processor (document collection)

Processor → Underwriter (file submission)

Underwriter → Attorney (clear-to-close notification)

Insurance Agent → Lender (binder delivery)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Loan estimates, closing disclosures, underwriting conditions, commitment letters

Consumed: Borrower financial documents, property details, appraisal reports, title commitments

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Repeated document requests from different departments

Unclear underwriting condition status

Appraisal scheduling delays

Insurance binder delivery timing misalignment

Last-minute funding conditions

7. Subcontractor Task Fulfillment
Ownership: Subcontractor (executor), Realtor/Seller (authorizer), Inspector (validator)

Entry Point
Repair request assignment after inspection

Pre-listing improvement work order

Key Decisions
Repair scope and pricing acceptance

Scheduling priority relative to transaction timeline

Quality verification method and acceptance criteria

Invoice submission and payment approval process

Role Handoffs
Realtor → Subcontractor (work order issuance)

Subcontractor → Inspector (re-inspection request)

Subcontractor → Title Company (lien waiver submission)

Subcontractor → Attorney (invoice for escrow payment)

Data Created/Consumed
Created: Estimates, work orders, completion photos, invoices, lien waivers

Consumed: Inspection reports, property access instructions, deadline constraints, payment terms

Moments of Friction to Eliminate
Unclear repair scope leading to change orders

Property access coordination difficulties

Delayed quality verification holding up contingencies

Payment processing delays affecting subcontractor cash flow

Missing lien waivers preventing closing

Cross-Journey Orchestration Principles
Critical Synchronization Points
Contract to Contingency: All parties must align on contingency clock start

Inspection to Repair: Repair scope must be agreed before contingency deadline

Appraisal to Renegotiation: Appraisal results may trigger price renegotiation

Clear-to-Close to Scheduling: All parties must coordinate closing date availability

Funding to Recording: Funding must precede recording, recording must precede possession

Data Integrity Requirements
Single source of truth for deadlines and milestone dates

Document version control with audit trail

Role-based access to sensitive financial information

Automated notification when dependent tasks complete

Friction Elimination Priorities
Automated deadline propagation when one date changes

Centralized document repository with role-specific views

Status dashboard showing entire transaction from multiple role perspectives

Communication audit trail preventing "he said/she said" disputes

Mobile-friendly task completion for field work (inspections, repairs, showings)

System Imperative: The CRM must make the complex feel coordinated, not chaotic. Every participant should know their responsibility, their timeline, and who to contact when questions arise—without overwhelming them with irrelevant information.
